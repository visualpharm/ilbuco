About Cariló

Cariló is a charming coastal town located in Buenos Aires Province, Argentina. Known for its extensive pine forests and beautiful beaches, it offers a unique blend of natural beauty and modern amenities. The name "Cariló" comes from the indigenous Mapuche language, meaning "green dune," which perfectly describes the area's distinctive landscape.

The town was designed as a resort community with strict building codes that preserve the natural environment. Streets wind through the forest, and homes are built to complement rather than dominate the landscape.

Il Buco's Setting

Our property is nestled in the pine forest, just a short 100-meter walk from the beach. This prime location offers guests the best of both worlds: the tranquility of the forest and the proximity to the ocean.

The building was designed in 2025 by a foreign architect using suspended concrete volumes and high-performance walls, creating a modern retreat that harmonizes with its natural surroundings.
